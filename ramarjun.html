Srinivasa Ramanujan

Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887â€“1920) was an Indian mathematical genius who made substantial contributions to number theory, continued fractions, and infinite series. Despite little formal education in mathematics, his self-taught skills amazed mathematicians worldwide.

Key Contributions:

  • Ramanujan Prime: A concept in prime number theory.
  • Ramanujan Theta Function: Used in partition functions and modular forms.
  • Mock Theta Functions: Later found essential in string theory and quantum physics.
  • Highly Composite Numbers: Advanced studies in divisor functions.

Famous Equation:

\[ 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \dots = -\frac{1}{12} \]

(This appears in string theory and quantum physics.)

Ramanujan's work was recognized by G. H. Hardy, who brought him to Cambridge University. His notebooks contained thousands of previously unknown results that are still being studied today.
