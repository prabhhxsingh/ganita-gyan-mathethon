Bhaskara II

Bhaskara II (1114–1185 CE) was a remarkable mathematician and astronomer. He made groundbreaking contributions in **algebra, calculus, and trigonometry**.

Key Contributions:

  • Differential Calculus: Predated Newton and Leibniz in derivatives.
  • Pi Approximation: Improved value of π with high precision.
  • Solutions to Pell’s Equation: Gave integer solutions for quadratic equations.
  • Astronomy: Calculated planetary positions accurately.

Famous Equation:

\[ x^2 - Ny^2 = 1 \]

(Bhaskara's contributions to Pell's Equation in number theory.)

His work **"Siddhanta Shiromani"** was a major mathematical text used for centuries.
