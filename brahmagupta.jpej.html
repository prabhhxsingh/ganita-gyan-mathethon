Brahmagupta

Brahmagupta (598â€“668 CE) was a pioneering mathematician and astronomer from India. He introduced **rules for zero and negative numbers**, which had a profound impact on mathematics.

Key Contributions:

  • Concept of Zero: First to define zero as a number and its mathematical operations.
  • Negative Numbers: Developed rules for operations with negative numbers.
  • Quadratic Equations: Provided general solutions for quadratic equations.
  • Gravity: Described the concept of gravity centuries before Newton.

Famous Equation:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

(Brahmagupta's method for solving quadratic equations.)

His book "Brahmasphutasiddhanta" was a landmark in mathematics, influencing scholars across the world.
