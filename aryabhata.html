Aryabhata

Aryabhata (476–550 CE) was one of the greatest Indian mathematicians and astronomers. His seminal work, the Aryabhatiya, introduced groundbreaking concepts in trigonometry, algebra, and astronomy.

Key Contributions:

  • Value of π (Pi): He approximated π to 3.1416 with great accuracy.
  • Zero and Place Value System: Helped develop the modern decimal system.
  • Earth’s Rotation: Stated that the Earth rotates on its axis, long before Copernicus.
  • Sine Table: Introduced trigonometric functions like sine in calculations.

Famous Equation:

\[ Sine \theta = \frac{Chord}{Diameter} \]

(Aryabhata defined the sine function in a way that influenced later mathematics.)

His contributions laid the foundation for advancements in **astronomy, calculus, and modern mathematics**.
